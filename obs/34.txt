====== 34. Jesus Puts Forth Other Parables ======



{{https://api.unfoldingword.org/obs/jpg/1/en/360px/obs-en-34-01.jpg}}

Jesus told them many stories about the realm of heaven. In the first, he compared the realm of heaven to a small kernel of mustard seed, which a man planted in his field. Now the mustard seed is the smallest of any seed.



{{https://api.unfoldingword.org/obs/jpg/1/en/360px/obs-en-34-02.jpg}}

As the plant grows, it will become as large as any tree. Even the birds can live in its branches.



{{https://api.unfoldingword.org/obs/jpg/1/en/360px/obs-en-34-03.jpg}}

Jesus gave another analogy, saying "God's way is like a woman who took some sourdough and mixed it with three loaved of flour. The sourdough flavored the entire batch of dough."



{{https://api.unfoldingword.org/obs/jpg/1/en/360px/obs-en-34-04.jpg}}

The realm of heaven is like a secret treasure hidden in a field. A man found it and then he hid it in the field again. He was so excited to have the treasure he sold everything he had and used the money to buy the field.



{{https://api.unfoldingword.org/obs/jpg/1/en/360px/obs-en-34-05.jpg}}

And God's realm is like a business man who is looking for valuable jewels. When he finds one, he goes and sells everything he has and buys it.



{{https://api.unfoldingword.org/obs/jpg/1/en/360px/obs-en-34-06.jpg}}

And he told a story to some men who thought more highly of themselves than others. These two men went to the temple to pray, a Pharisee who was a religious leader of the day and a publican.



{{https://api.unfoldingword.org/obs/jpg/1/en/360px/obs-en-34-07.jpg}}

And the Pharisee stood, and prayed by himself these things, and said, God, I do thankings to thee, for I am not as other men, raveners, unjust, adulterers, as also this publican;



{{https://api.unfoldingword.org/obs/jpg/1/en/360px/obs-en-34-08.jpg}}

Two days a week I don't eat enything at all. I regularly give away a part of my stuff.



{{https://api.unfoldingword.org/obs/jpg/1/en/360px/obs-en-34-09.jpg}}

But the tax collector remained far off, unable to lift up his eyes to heaven, and beat his chest in sorrow. He pleaded for God to have mercy on him, a sinner.



{{https://api.unfoldingword.org/obs/jpg/1/en/360px/obs-en-34-10.jpg}}

Let me make this really clear. This man is the one who went home forgiven by God instead of the other one. For the person who thinks he is better than others will be made humble, but the person who is humble will be built up.



// A Bible story from: Matthew 13:31-33, 44-46; Mark 4:30-32; Luke 13:18-21; 18:9-14 //
